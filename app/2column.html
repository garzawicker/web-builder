Decorated designer

She is an incredible designer who consistently creates amazing solutions

Emily Davies | May 17, 2016

After just a few months working for Met Media, Kristen Morrison, a communication design major slated to graduate in December 2016, felt like she was at the top of her class – at least in terms of what she was learning. Because of her job, Morrison was already familiar with many of the design tools covered in her classes.

“Feeling and being ahead of the game has not only allowed me to thrive when it comes to my design coursework, but it has drastically improved my confidence in myself and what I do,” said Morrison.

And with seven national student design awards in her pocket, including being named second best designer in the country from College Media Business and Advertising Managers, Morrison is definitely thriving.

“She is an incredible designer who consistently creates amazing solutions with an eye for design that is extremely advanced for how far along she is in school,” said Kathleen Jewby, production manager at Met Media.

Now a senior designer after two years with Met Media, Morrison trains junior designers and works with internal and external clients both on and off campus to create advertising, identity systems and marketing materials.

Take Met Media’s media kit, the first project Morrison was assigned when she started the job. It’s one of her favorite projects not only because of what she learned but because she was able to take charge of the whole design. And her hard work paid off, winning herself and Met Media a first place 2015 Associated Collegiate Press award for a rate card and a second place 2015 College Media Association Pinnacle award for best rate cardhttps://msudenver.edu/media kit.

Or, another example, the 24th annual Martin Luther King Jr. Peace Breakfast. Morrison said she was honored to create the event’s logo, program, marketing materials and an advertisement, which won her three awards in 2015: a first place CMBAM award for color display ad, and two second place display ad awards from ACP and CMA.

Yet despite all the recognition her hard work has given her, Morrison said winning the awards is not her main goal.

“I just find myself driven to put out my best work, no matter what the project,” Morrison said. “I feel really humbled by it all, and I’m really grateful to have been given such amazing opportunities.”

And she said she has Met Media to thank for that.

“I know that without working there, and working with such a talented group of people, I would not be in the position I am today,” said Morrison. “I’d especially like to thank all the graphic designers I’ve worked with throughout my time there. Every single one of them has been so beyond talented, and it pushes me to try and be better every day.”
